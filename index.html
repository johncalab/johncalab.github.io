About

I am a mathematician and I am currently an Insight data science fellow in New York.

I completed my undergraduate studies in math at the university of Pisa. I started my PhD at the university of Sheffield under the supervision of Tom Bridgeland, but I earned my degree from the university of Oxford (which technically means I don’t really have a PhD), with a focus on algebraic geometry.

For a brief period I was a Doctoral Prize Fellow at Imperial College London, and afterwards I spent a few years at Rice university as an Instructor and NSF fellow. There I essentially covered all the responsibilities of a professor (minus the dreaded committee work). At the end of my time at Rice, I decided to switch field, despite having received offers to be a tenure-track assistant professor. I was briefly a Research Investigator at MD Anderson, where I started to learn about machine learning and AI.

Here are a short résumé and a longer (and outdated) academic CV.
